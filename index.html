Core Research Pillars

Focus Areas

What We Investigate

& Food Safety">
Area 01
Non-thermal Plasma & Food Safety
Focus
Microbial inactivation of pathogens in both planktonic state and biofilm using atmospheric plasma; plasma decontamination of pesticides from fresh produce; plasma inactivation mechanism via individual radicals generated by plasma on microbes.
Methods
Atmospheric plasma (DBD), submerged plasma reactors, biofilm assays, SEM/EDX, ATR-FTIR, HPLC, LC-MS.
Why It Matters
Non-thermal plasma is a powerful chemical-free tool for food decontamination, extending shelf life while preserving nutritional quality and avoiding harmful residues.
& Aerosol Science">
Area 02
Single Particle Analysis (SPA) & Aerosol Science
Focus
Establishment of Single Particle Analytical (SPA) technique using SEM/EDX and ATR-FTIR in combination for rapid microbial detection and environmental aerosol characterisation, including asbestos mineral analysis.
Methods
Low-Z particle EPMA, ATR-FTIR imaging, ED-EPMA, quantitative single-particle analysis.
Why It Matters
Asbestos (Mg-silicate minerals) is a cause of mesothelioma and a potential health risk in developing countries; accurate characterisation is essential for environmental health protection.
Area 03
Environmental Contaminant & Pesticide Degradation
Focus
Study of pesticide removal and degradation pathways in water using microplasma; identification of byproducts by LC-MS; mechanistic and kinetic studies of contaminant breakdown.
Methods
Microplasma discharge, LC-MS, ozonation, role-of-radical analysis, kinetic modelling.
Why It Matters
Pesticide contamination of water threatens ecosystems and human health; understanding degradation mechanisms enables more effective water treatment strategies.
Area 04
Food Chemistry & Quality Analysis
Focus
Evaluation of chemical quality of plasma-treated food — including flavonoid and ascorbic acid extraction — and physical quality changes of treated food samples.
Methods
HPLC, LC-MS, UV-Vis spectrophotometry, extraction workflows, quality assays.
Why It Matters
Links environmental exposures and food processing interventions to clinical and nutritional outcomes, supporting evidence-based food safety policy.
Area 05
Analytical Method Development
Focus
Build reliable, reproducible analytical protocols for complex environmental, biological, and food matrices. Method validation across a range of compound classes.
Methods
Full validation (precision, linearity, LOD/LOQ), certified reference materials, QC samples, SPE clean-up.
Why It Matters
Robust methods make results trustworthy, publishable, and reproducible — underpinning all downstream research conclusions.

About
Dr. Muhammad Saiful Islam Khan is an Associate Professor at the School of Medicine, Central Asian University. His research focuses on non-thermal plasma sterilisation, food safety, single particle analysis, pesticide degradation, and advanced analytical chemistry including HPLC and LC-MS.
Research Areas
Analytical Chemistry
HPLC and LC-MS/MS method development and validation for environmental and biological matrices.
Environmental Biomarkers
Profiling oxidative stress and exposure biomarkers in human populations across Central Asia.
Food Safety
Detection of pesticide residues, contaminants, and phenolic compounds in food and water.
Oxidative Stress
Quantitative analysis of reactive oxygen species and antioxidant capacity in clinical samples.
Sample Preparation
SPE, QuEChERS, and advanced extraction workflows for complex sample matrices.
Collaborative Research
Cross-disciplinary partnerships with clinical, public health, and industrial institutions.
