What We Do

Core Research Pillars

Three interconnected domains of environmental health science driving our work.

Exposure & Health

We study how chemical, biological, and physical exposures affect human health through targeted assays and screening workflows.

Analytical Science

We use HPLC and LC-MS/MS workflows with rigorous quality control to quantify and identify compounds in environmental and biological matrices.

Translation & Collaboration

We connect laboratory methods with real-world questions in medicine, public health, and food safety through active partnerships.

About
Prof. Khurshid Nosirov is the Principal Investigator of the Environmental Health Research Group at the Advanced Technology Center, Tashkent Institute of Chemical Technology. His research focuses on the development and application of advanced analytical methods — particularly HPLC and LC-MS/MS — for the detection of environmental contaminants, food safety monitoring, and the profiling of oxidative stress biomarkers in human populations.
Research Areas
Analytical Chemistry
HPLC and LC-MS/MS method development and validation for environmental and biological matrices.
Environmental Biomarkers
Profiling oxidative stress and exposure biomarkers in human populations across Central Asia.
Food Safety
Detection of pesticide residues, contaminants, and phenolic compounds in food and water samples.
Oxidative Stress
Quantitative analysis of reactive oxygen species and antioxidant capacity in clinical samples.
Sample Preparation
SPE, QuEChERS, and other advanced extraction workflows for complex sample matrices.
Collaborative Research
Cross-disciplinary partnerships with clinical, public health, and industrial research institutions.
